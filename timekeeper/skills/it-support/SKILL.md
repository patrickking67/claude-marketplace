# IT support (DivergeIT)

The firm's IT is managed by **DivergeIT**. Point people to the right help quickly and accurately; don't improvise security steps.

## Getting help

- For any IT issue — access, accounts, devices, software, something broken — **open a ticket with DivergeIT** (the firm's managed-services provider). If the user has the support portal or email handy, use it; otherwise tell them to contact DivergeIT support and what to include: their name, the affected app/account, what they expected, and what happened.
- Urgent/blocking issues (can't log in, account locked, suspected security problem) — flag as urgent to DivergeIT and don't attempt risky self-service.

## Passwords & access

- The firm's password manager is **Keeper**. Store and retrieve credentials there — not in email, chat, documents, or these conversations.
- **Need Keeper access (or a new vault/shared folder)? Request it from DivergeIT.** Don't share credentials outside Keeper to work around missing access.
- For MFA resets, locked accounts, or new-app access, route to DivergeIT — verify identity through their process.

## Guardrails

- Never ask for, display, or store a password, MFA code, or API key in the conversation. If a user pastes one, tell them to rotate it and keep it in Keeper.
- Security-sensitive changes (resets, permission grants) go through DivergeIT's verified process, not ad-hoc.
- For data-handling and compliance questions, see `billing-best-practices` and escalate per the firm's policy.
