# Billing description style

Entries land on client invoices and, in receivership/court-supervised matters, in front of a judge ruling on fees. Write every `note` to survive a § 330 fee audit.

## Voice

- **Present-tense billing verb first:** Review, Draft, Revise, Correspond, Email, Prepare, Coordinate, Forward, Finalize, Discuss, Confer, Provide, Process, Analyze, Research, Attend, Confirm, Request, Circulate, Hand-deliver.
- **Name the thing and the person** by **full name** where the source gives it ("Peggy Lennon," "Brian Landau," "Ori Blumenfeld"). Never "counsel," "the broker," "the bank rep" when a name is available. No firm names needed.
- **One discrete task = one entry.** No first person. No filler ("briefly," "quickly," "just," "worked on," "attention to").
- **Chain same-issue steps** with `;`. A description that bundles **different topics** (watch for "; and") must **split** into separate entries.
- **No privileged substance** — describe the work, not confidential strategy.

### Real-shaped examples (present tense)

- `Email all leases to Xochitl Cortez`
- `Prepare updated rent roll; send rent roll and responses to questions to Xochitl Cortez`
- `Respond to email from Steve Donell re unfunded security deposits pertaining to PSA`
- `Review and analyze financial statements submitted by Adrienne Houle; prepare summary of findings for court report`
- `Telephone conference with Brian Landau re status of receivership proceedings and upcoming hearing; discuss strategy and next steps`

## Increments & honesty

- Tenths of an hour, **round UP**: 1–6 min = 0.1, 7–12 = 0.2, 13–18 = 0.3, … Minimum 0.1.
- **Honest estimate of actual time** — never pad to a daily target (e.g. 8 hrs), never infer hours from email volume (10 short emails on one issue ≠ 1.0 hr), never trim below what the task took. Meetings/hearings use **actual** calendar duration.
- Email review/short replies trend 0.1–0.3; substantive drafting 0.4–1.5.

## No block billing

Courts routinely disallow block-billed receiver fees. ❌ *"Drafted fee application; forwarded tax returns; prepared spreadsheet; sent monthly statement"* = four entries, not one.

## § 330(a)(3)–(4) — test every entry

(i) benefit to the estate; (ii) necessary to administration; (iii) no unnecessary duplication (reflect the biller's **distinct** contribution); (iv) time proportional to complexity. Fail or doubt → **flag** with a § 330 note; never silently include or drop. Full skip list and the four-prong test: `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/non-billable.md`.
